# Decision Structure

This document explains how organizations define decision authority and escalation paths within an operating model.

Clear decision structures allow organizations to move quickly while maintaining accountability.

---

## Decision Authority

Effective operating models define who is responsible for making decisions at different levels of the organization.

Typical levels include:

- executive leadership
- governance committees
- program leadership
- delivery teams

Each level should understand:

- what decisions they can make
- when escalation is required
- how decisions are communicated

---

## Escalation Paths

Escalation paths ensure that complex issues are resolved efficiently.

Issues should escalate when:

- cross-team alignment cannot be reached
- risks threaten delivery timelines
- strategic priorities conflict
- resource constraints require leadership intervention

---

## Decision Documentation

Organizations benefit from maintaining a decision log documenting:

- key decisions
- rationale
- stakeholders involved
- expected outcomes
