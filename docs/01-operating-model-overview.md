# Enterprise Operating Model Overview

This document explains the purpose of an enterprise operating model and how organizations structure leadership, governance, and delivery to execute strategy.

Organizations often struggle to execute transformation initiatives not because the strategy is unclear, but because the organizational structure required to support execution is not defined.

An enterprise operating model defines how leadership, governance, programs, and delivery teams coordinate to translate strategy into execution.

---

## Core Components of an Operating Model

A practical operating model typically includes five elements:

### Strategic Direction

Leadership defines strategic objectives and priorities that guide organizational investments and initiatives.

### Organizational Structure

Teams are organized around functions, capabilities, or value streams aligned to strategic goals.

### Governance and Decision Authority

Decision-making authority is clearly defined to ensure accountability and reduce delays.

### Execution Coordination

Programs and initiatives are coordinated across teams with shared milestones and delivery goals.

### Measurement and Outcomes

Leadership tracks progress using metrics that measure both execution progress and strategic impact.

---

## Why Operating Models Matter

Organizations without a defined operating model often experience:

- unclear ownership of initiatives
- overlapping responsibilities
- slow decision-making
- misalignment between leadership and delivery teams
- difficulty scaling transformation efforts

A structured operating model helps organizations create alignment between strategy, governance, and execution.

## Relationship to the Transformation Operating System

This repository is part of the broader **Transformation Operating System** framework.
